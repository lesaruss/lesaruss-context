# Russell's Roving Reporters Cartridge

## What This Is
Russell's Roving Reporters (RRR) is a student journalism and real-world reporting brand. A K12 Unlocked client. Kids are the reporters, districts are the sponsors, LESARUSS is the production and distribution platform. Waiting room staged for 4/20 soft launch, activation follows K12 Unlocked district deals.

## Domain
- Primary URL: `russellsrovingreporters.com`
- Sits under K12 Unlocked as a client, pairs with Chester Is Cool as the real-kid counterpart

## Key People
- **Brandon Cole** — K12 Unlocked GM (RRR's umbrella). Accountability owner.
- **Sean A. Russell** — Founder. Roving Reporters concept came from Sean's district background.
- **Jasmine Laurent** — CCO, owns brand voice standard

## The Product
Real student reporters covering their schools, districts, and communities. Output: short-form video stories, written pieces, podcast segments. Distribution: school district sites (K12 Unlocked platform), GeekFon Radio podcast network, Chester is Cool cross-promotion for younger grades.

Tiered model:
- Student reporter program (inside a district)
- District-branded RRR chapter (middle + high school)
- Cross-district features (national stories)

## Overlap With Other Brands
- **K12 Unlocked** — parent umbrella. Districts sponsor RRR chapters as part of K12 Unlocked deals.
- **Chester Is Cool** — sibling K12 client. Chester is animated fiction for younger grades, RRR is real kids reporting for middle + high school. Shared brand site showcases student media work.
- **BCPS** — natural first client. Brandon Graham + Broward district content teams.
- **GeekFon Society** — RRR podcast segments feed GeekFon Radio as dedicated shows.
- **Mogul in the Making** — cross-format handshake. RRR student reporters could interview Sean on tour, Mogul crew cameos in district segments.
- **Sean A. Russell** — founder credibility and origin story.

## Rules
- COPPA + FERPA compliance baseline on all student-facing content
- Districts own their students' likeness and footage, contracts define distribution rights explicitly
- Always "Russell's Roving Reporters" or "RRR," never "Roving Reporters" alone
- Student content routed through district review before publish
- No em-dashes, ADA compliance on every surface, captions on all video at publish time

## How to Apply
When any agent touches RRR content, district partnerships, or student media, load this cartridge plus `cartridges/brands/k12-unlocked.md`.
